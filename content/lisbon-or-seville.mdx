Short answer: pick **Seville for a 2-day trip**, **Lisbon for 3 days or more**. Seville is compact enough to feel finished in a weekend. Lisbon keeps unlocking neighborhoods the longer you stay, and it punishes rushed visits with its hills.

## The 60-second comparison

| | Lisbon | Seville |
|---|---|---|
| Ideal length | 3 days | 2 days |
| Feels like | Atlantic light, hills, tiles | Andalusian courtyards, orange trees |
| Budget per day (mid) | ~140 EUR | ~120 EUR |
| Summer heat | Warm but breezy | Brutal, often 38°C+ |
| Winter | Mild, rainy spells | Mild and often sunny |
| Getting around | Hilly, trams and funiculars help | Flat, walk everywhere |
| Airport to center | Metro, ~25 min | Bus, ~35 min |

## When Lisbon wins

Lisbon wins on variety. You get viewpoint mornings in [Alfama](/lisbon/where-to-stay#alfama), a maritime afternoon in Belém, and a completely different night out in Bairro Alto, all inside one trip. The food scene runs deeper too, from one-euro pastéis de nata to serious seafood.

It also wins in summer. When Seville hits 40°C in July, Lisbon's Atlantic breeze keeps afternoons workable. If your dates are locked to July or August, that alone decides it.

Start with the [3-day Lisbon itinerary](/lisbon/3-days) to see how the days fit together.

## When Seville wins

Seville wins on intensity per hour. The Alcázar, the cathedral and Plaza de España sit within a 20-minute walk of each other, so a weekend actually covers the headline sights without rushing. It is flat, which your legs will notice after any Lisbon trip.

It also wins in winter. From November to February, Seville regularly serves sunny 17°C days while most of Europe is grey. For a January morale trip, there is no contest.

The [2-day Seville itinerary](/seville/2-days) shows exactly what fits.

## The trap to avoid in both

Booking sights on arrival. The Alcázar in Seville and the Jerónimos Monastery in Lisbon both sell out days ahead in season. Book timed entries before you fly, then build the rest of the day around them.

## Verdict

- Weekend of 2 days: **Seville**
- 3 to 4 days: **Lisbon**
- July or August dates: **Lisbon**
- November to February dates: **Seville**
